Projects I've Done

  • August 2017

    As a student in the Girls Who Code Summer Immersion program, I worked with a team to build a website that guides teens through interacting with their Autistic siblings

  • March 2018

    At HackNYU 2018, our team was one of the top 3 Finalists in HackNYU's Accessibility category. Our project, EchoJournal, helps parents of kids with autism decipher their child's echolalic behaviors. I contributed towards storyboarding and pitching the project

  • September 2018

    At HackCMU 2018, our team won First Place Overall for Bitread, a Python project that teaches people with autism how to read. I implemented the Text-to-Speech portion of the project, storyboarded it, and pitched the project to judges.

  • November 2018

    At Hack112 2018, our team won First Place in the Best Social Good category for Prescriptime, a Python project that enables patients with Alzheimer's to take their medications on time. I implemented OpenCV, a computer vision library that distinguishes the user's pill bottle when they hold it up to their laptop web camera.

  • December 2018

    I spent three weeks building Virtuoso, a Guitar-Hero like video game for violists. I designed and implemented my own graphics for the game, used Aubio and Pyaudio to extract frequencies from the user's viola, and OpenCV to extract notes in order from a piece of online sheet music.

  • February 2019

    At TartanHacks 2019, our team was among the top 5 Finalists for ConvoCoach, a Python project that teaches people with autism how to have conversations. I implemented the Text-to-Speech and Speech-to-Text portions of the project, storyboarded it, and pitched the project to judges.
