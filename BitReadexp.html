BitRead

I first started this project back in September 2018, at HackCMU. Our team built it in Python, and its purpose was to guide kids with autism through learning how to read in a way no other program had before.

What made our project unique was the fact that broke sentences down into words and phrases for the user to master, verbally tested the user on those words/phrases, evaluated user responses, and provided real-time feedback to the user. The reasoning behind this was that when reading individual words in a sentence, kids with autism often forget the words that precede the word they're currently reading, which makes putting the words together a challenge.

We used Google Cloud's Text-to-Speech and Speech-to-Text APIs, Pyaudio, and Pygame to incorporate all of these features. I was in charge of integrating the Text-to-Speech portion, which would spell out the words the user was being tested on, prompt the user for answers, and give verbal feedback.

Currently, I'm trying to turn BitRead into a website, designing a cleaner UI and adding features that will help users' reading comprehension. I'm aiming to test this website in classrooms once it's finished the Beta stage, and will be linking it below once it's ready.
